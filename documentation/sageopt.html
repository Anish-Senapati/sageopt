Overview of sageopt¶

Sageopt includes functionality for optimization and checking nonnegativity of signomials and polynomials. The conceptual components here are:

  1. Some classes representing signomials and polynomials, as well as domains to which these functions are restricted.

  2. A high-level modeling interface (called “coniclifts”) for convex optimization problems. This interface includes primal and dual “SAGE constraints.”

Sageopt then takes these two components, and defines various functions to reduce user’s barrier to entry in working with SAGE relaxations.

Our main concern is to make it easier to get started with optimization. SAGE relaxations for optimization have different “primal” and “dual” forms, and it would take a bit of expertise to implement the advanced dual-form relaxations from scratch.
